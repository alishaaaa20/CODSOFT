Tribute to Four Martyrs of Nepal

The celebration of the lives and sacrifices of Dharma Bhakta Mathema, Shukraraj Shastri, Dashrath Chand, and Gangalal Shrestha is often observed on Martyrs' Day in Nepal. This significant day falls on Magh 16th of the Nepali calendar, which usually corresponds to the end of January in the Gregorian calendar.



  • Shukraraj Shastri (1917-1941)

    Shukraraj Shastri, a prominent figure in the 1950 revolution against the Rana regime, displayed unwavering courage in the face of oppression. As a scholar and patriot, he faced a firing squad in Sundhara, Kathmandu, marking a crucial turning point in Nepal's quest for democracy.

  • Dharma Bhakta Mathema (1894-1941)

    Dharma Bhakta Mathema dedicated his life to advocating educational reforms and social justice. Leading the 1951 education reform movement, his commitment to quality education for all left an indelible impact. His execution during the revolution emphasized his resolute dedication to the values he championed.

  • Dashrath Chand (1912-1941)

    Dashrath Chand, an instrumental figure in the fight against the autocratic Rana rule, contributed significantly to the 1950 revolution. His commitment to democratic ideals and sacrifices made during the revolutionary period exemplify his pivotal role in shaping Nepal's political landscape.

  • Gangalal Shrestha (1913-1941)

    Ganga Lal Shrestha, a key leader in the Nepali Congress movement, played a crucial role in challenging the Rana regime. His leadership during the 1950 revolution was instrumental in establishing democracy in Nepal. Facing the gallows, Shrestha's sacrifice symbolizes resilience and determination for a free and democratic Nepal.


Together, Shukraraj Shastri, Dharma Bhakta Mathema, Dashrath Chand, and Ganga Lal Shrestha form a collective legacy of courage and dedication that continues to inspire generations in Nepal.
